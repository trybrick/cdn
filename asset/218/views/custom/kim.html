Chef Bill Kim realized early that his passion for cooking lies with his Korean heritage. He opened his first restaurant, Urbanbelly, that spawned a foodie fan base around Chicago. Since then, with the help of his wife, he’s opened two more restaurants and launched a line of Asian sauces. Bill’s ultimate goal is to break boundaries with home cooks and demystify Asian cooking.

Q & A

Which Mariano’s do you shop?

West Loop

What does the restaurant name Belly represent?

Belly is comforting. All humans come from the belly. So the word Belly means something approachable and comforting.

Where do you begin if you’re new to Asian cooking?

First you have to get used to tasting all these sauces, seeking out certain brands. So if you’re using soy sauce, use gluten-free soy sauce.

Why gluten-free soy sauce?

I like to stay preservative-free as much as possible. I created all of our retail sauces to compete with others who have preservatives.

What’s the easiest Korean dish to make?

Bibimbap. It’s rice with chili sauce on top and a fried egg.

Is there a simple tip for people to improve their Asian cooking?

Most people cook with sesame oil. I don’t. I think it’s more of a finishing oil. You don’t really cook with it. You flavor items with it.

What oil do you prefer to cook with at home?

Grapeseed. I use a lot of extra virgin olive oil at home too.

Favorite beverage to pair with Asian food?

I drink mostly beer or cider. I LOVE Hitochino Nest, a Japanese beer. It is THE best beer. And it’s here at Mariano’s.
